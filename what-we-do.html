01

The First Look

Voice, listings, and first impressions

Do you sound like yourself before anyone meets you?

Best for

Your business feels scattered before people even reach your website — different surfaces, different voices, none of it quite matching who you actually are.

What it does

Defines how your business sounds and rewrites every place it shows up in the world to match. Voice and presence, made consistent.

Can include

Voice and tone guide, Google Business Profile, social bios, directory listings, taglines, email signature, short descriptions.

Natural next step

Often leads into The Window or The Touch-Up.

02

The Window

Website, landing pages, and the path in

Does it invite people in?

Best for

People find you, but the website doesn't quite help them understand, trust, or take the next step.

What it does

Clarifies the digital storefront and the path from interest to action. Makes it easier for the right people to say yes.

Can include

Website builds or refreshes, landing pages, contact forms, page copy, calls to action, search visibility basics.

Natural next step

Often pairs with The Welcome Mat.

03

The Welcome Mat

First replies, welcome emails, and early follow-up

Does the first moment feel right?

Best for

People inquire, sign up, or book — but the first thing they hear back feels thin, cold, or like it came from a different business entirely.

What it does

Shapes the first 24 to 48 hours after someone arrives. The impression that sets the tone for everything that follows.

Can include

Welcome emails, inquiry replies, onboarding flows, auto-responders, confirmation messages, contact form follow-ups.

Natural next step

Pairs well with The Window and The Open Door.

04

The Open Door

Newsletters, content, and ongoing relationship

Are you still showing up after the welcome?

Best for

You have past customers, warm leads, or interested people who already know you — but no steady rhythm for staying connected with them.

What it does

Builds the inside-the-relationship rhythms that keep the business present long after the first hello — for people who have already come in.

Can include

Newsletters, customer emails, content systems, retention sequences, recurring communication, light campaign planning.

Natural next step

Natural bridge into The Touch-Up.

05

The Touch-Up

Monthly continuity and upkeep

Is anyone keeping it sharp?

Best for

Things have been built, but no one is keeping them current, aligned, or reflective of the business as it continues to grow and change.

What it does

Keeps the in-market experience from quietly drifting out of date. Maintains what has been built — light touch through full care.

Can include

Copy refreshes, small website edits, seasonal updates, voice checks, listing updates, offer updates, regular review.

Natural next step

The primary ongoing offer. Usually follows any build engagement.

Common starting points.

These are the shapes the work tends to take. Not packages — just common combinations that solve recognisable problems.

First impression reset

The First Look + The Window

Public-facing presence feels scattered, outdated, or like a different business than the one you're actually running.

Inquiry flow fix

The Window + The Welcome Mat

Getting traffic or inquiries but losing momentum somewhere between interest and action.

Customer communication rhythm

The Welcome Mat + The Open Door

Need a better first hello and a reason to keep showing up after it.

Build and keep it current

Any project + The Touch-Up

Want the pieces to stay aligned after the initial work is done, without having to manage it yourself.

The whole in-market experience

All five, together

Want the full customer-facing path looked at as one connected thing. A recommended approach for a full reset.
