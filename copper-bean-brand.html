Brand Identity Exploration

Four logo directions, two font pairings per direction, and three colour schemes — exploring how the brand could look and feel before anything gets locked.

01

Logo directions

Four typographic approaches, each setting a different tone. All work with the "+" as a copper accent — the constant across directions.

Primary mark

The main mark. Bold condensed type in a copper-bordered frame — confident, structured, reads clearly at any size. The "+" in copper anchors the centre. Built for signage, menus, packaging, and anywhere the brand needs to lead.

Alternate — Monogram

The shorthand. "C+B" in copper brackets — works small (favicons, cup stamps, social avatars, tags) where the full name won't fit. The brackets frame it like something worth keeping.

Alternate — Horizontal

The wide format. Same type, stretched into a single line between copper rules — for headers, window decals, aprons, and anywhere the layout runs horizontal. Like a nameplate on a door.

04

Our recommendation

Where we'd take it, and why.

Selected direction

Primary mark (Impact, square frame) + Pairing A (Impact / DM Sans) + Classic palette.

Impact gives the brand its presence — bold, condensed, impossible to miss. It reads like signage even on a screen, which is exactly right for a coffee bar that's confident about what it does. Paired with DM Sans for body text, the heavy headlines and clean body copy create a natural contrast: loud where it matters, quiet everywhere else. The classic palette (bean, copper, cream, stone) keeps it grounded in real materials.
