A Someday Project

It started with a pile of leather under the tree.

The tools arrived at Christmas, and my husband Chris was instantly enamoured. It was the kind of new-hobby spark where you can already picture the whole workshop.

He started dreaming about the bags he'd make someday. So I started dreaming too, and did the part I love best. I gave the someday a name, a raven and peony crest, a voice, and a whole little world to live in.

Word has it the launch was meant to be summer 2026, which gives him roughly a fortnight to master an entire craft. No pressure. Until then, the brand is built and waiting, ready for the day his real work is good enough to photograph.

the someday collection

Five pieces, five ways of moving through the world.

Each named ahead of the bench, with the words written before the leather. The descriptions below are the brand's own voice.

The Watch

For those who notice.

storm blue · ash violet · antique brass

Structured and quietly powerful, The Watch is made for a life lived with intention. Crafted in deep Storm Blue leather, it reads nearly black in shadow, revealing its depth only in the light. Ash Violet details appear sparingly, a subtle signal of intellect rather than ornament.

Designed to carry only what matters, a notebook, a book, a phone, it resists excess by design. The antique brass closure grounds the piece in tradition, evoking objects meant to endure, not impress.

Composed, observant, and deliberate, The Watch is a companion for those who move through the world attentively.

The Hearth

For those who gather life as they go.

blush pink · soft taupe · antique brass

Soft, grounded, and quietly generous, The Hearth is made for everyday life, the kind that carries a little more than expected. Crafted in Light Rose leather, the body feels warm and human, never precious. Soft Taupe at the base and handles anchors the piece, giving it weight, balance, and longevity.

This is a bag meant to live by the door and be reached for without thinking. It holds notebooks, gloves, small necessities, and the unremarkable rituals that make up a day. The brass hardware brings a sense of continuity, evoking objects that are used often and kept close.

The Hearth is steady, familiar, and enduring, made beautiful through use but built to hold the abundance of an ordinary day.

The Archive

For what you carry forward.

caramel · timeworn black · antique brass

The Archive is a study in permanence. Tall, open, and unguarded, it is designed for books without dust jackets, papers with history, and objects that gain meaning through use. There is no zipper by design, nothing between you and what you carry.

Crafted in warm Caramel leather with black handles and straps, the silhouette is clean, upright, and intentional. Antique brass hardware grounds the piece in tradition, while the open form recalls library totes, field bags, and working satchels made to be reached into again and again.

The Archive belongs where books are stacked, candles burn low, and time moves slowly.

The Field

Made to move.

muted olive · warm caramel · antique brass

The Field is a working bag, shaped for motion rather than presentation. Its low, generous form is designed to be worn crossbody, set down in the grass, and lifted again without thought. Crafted in Muted Olive leather with Warm Caramel accents, it feels grounded, capable, and quietly resilient.

Soft by design, The Field moves with the body. It carries layers, tools, gathered things, whatever the day requires, without insisting on order or restraint.

The Field is not a bag for stillness. It is a bag for walking, working, and returning home with something in your hands.

The Keepsake

For what you carry forward.

pebble · warm driftwood · gold

The Keepsake is a small bag with a gentle presence, designed to be carried close, both in form and in feeling. Crafted in soft Pebble leather with subtle Warm Driftwood accents, it feels light in the hand and intimate in scale.

This is a bag for the in-between moments: a walk through wildflowers, a quiet afternoon, a shared picnic, a memory still unfolding. It holds only what is essential, a phone, a notebook, a few treasured things, and nothing that distracts from the moment itself.

The Keepsake is meant to accompany joy quietly, offering grace rather than statement. Built to hold what is cherished and delicate.
