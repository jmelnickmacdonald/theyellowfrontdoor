Fin & Oak Bark Bakery

Brand Identity · Website · Voice · In-Market System

Fin & Oak came to us with a name, a kitchen, and a mom-and-daughter team who bake pet treats in the Annapolis Valley. Everything else needed building. The brief was "make it feel like us" — which turned out to mean warm, a little chaotic, and nothing that looked like it was pulled off a shelf.

We built the brand identity, wrote and designed the full brand book, built the website, set the voice and tone, and wrote all the customer-facing copy. The test for every piece was the same: does this sound like the people who actually run the bakery?

Brand Book Website Voice & Tone Customer Communications Visual Identity

The brand book has the full system. The site is live and taking orders. And when you land on it, it sounds like the two people who run it — which was the whole point.

Copper + Bean

Voice & Messaging · Brand Identity · Landing Page · Concept

We invented Copper + Bean to demonstrate the full pipeline. A sharp modern coffee bar in Halifax's north end, run by two people who left corporate after the kids moved out. We wrote the brief ourselves and worked it all the way through from voice to screen. The point was to show what it looks like when voice, identity, and execution are built as one connected thing.

Voice came first: who are these people, how do they talk, what would they never say? The visual identity grew out of that — logo explorations, font pairings, colour palettes. Then we built a full landing page that puts both into practice with real copy.

Voice & Messaging Guide Logo Suite Font Pairings Colour Palettes Landing Page Menu

Three deliverables that read as one thing. The voice guide, the brand identity, and the landing page all connect — you can trace the decisions from one to the next.

Strong Ground

30-Day Social Strategy · Content Planning · Concept

Strong Ground is a fictional wellness coaching business we built as a strategy demonstration. The brief: a solo practitioner with a strong in-person reputation, no real social presence, and no idea where to start. We built the 30-day content plan that would get her there.

Screenshot

Voice guide first, then a content calendar with post types, topics, captions, and cadence mapped across 30 days. The goal was a plan she could hand to anyone and have it sound like her — not like a content template.

Lydia Nichols — Email System

Client Welcome Sequence · Referral Email · Concept

Most real estate agents go quiet the moment a listing is signed. We built an email system for Lydia Nichols — a fictional Annapolis Valley agent — that keeps clients informed, warm, and confident through the whole process. The brief was to sound like a person, not a drip campaign.

Screenshot

A five-email welcome and seller onboarding sequence, plus a referral email designed to generate warm introductions without feeling like a ask. Every email is written to feel like it came from Lydia specifically — her voice, her market, her values.
