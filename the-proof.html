Fin & Oak Bark Bakery

Client

Brand Identity · Website · Voice · In-Market System

Fin & Oak came to us with a name, a kitchen, and a mom-and-daughter team who bake pet treats in the Annapolis Valley. Everything else needed building. The brief was "make it feel like us," which turned out to mean warm, a little chaotic, and nothing that looked like it was pulled off a shelf.

We built the brand identity, wrote and designed the full brand book, built the website, set the voice and tone, and wrote all the customer-facing communications (welcome emails, order confirmations, the lot). The test for every piece was the same: does this sound like the people who actually run the bakery?

Brand Book Website Voice + Tone Customer Communications Visual Identity

The brand book has the full system. The site is live and taking orders. And when you land on it, it sounds like the two people who run it, which was the whole point.

Copper + Bean

Concept Piece

Voice & Messaging · Brand Identity · Landing Page

We invented Copper + Bean to demonstrate the full pipeline. It's a concept piece: a sharp modern coffee bar in Halifax's north end, run by two people who left corporate after the kids moved out. We wrote the brief ourselves and then worked it all the way through from voice to screen.

The point was to show what it looks like when voice, identity, and execution are built as one connected thing rather than three separate jobs.

Voice came first: who are these people, how do they talk, what would they never say? The visual identity grew out of that: logo explorations, font pairings, colour palettes, a recommended direction with rationale. Then we built a full landing page that puts both into practice with real copy and real photography.

Voice & Messaging Guide Logo Suite (3 Marks) Font Pairings Colour Palettes Landing Page

Three deliverables that read as one thing. The voice guide, the brand identity exploration, and the landing page all connect, and you can trace the decisions from one to the next.

Raven House Atelier

Concept Piece

Brand Identity · Style Guide · Lookbook

This one doesn't exist as a business. It's a personal project we built to explore what a quiet, craft-focused leather-goods brand could look and feel like. The imagery is AI-generated (some of the quirks are intentional, some we just liked), and the whole identity was designed from nothing as an exercise in restraint.

Lookbook cover

Visual direction, colour palette, typography, a style guide with usage rules, and a lookbook showing the brand in context. The voice is quiet on purpose: it talks about materials and craft, not lifestyle or aspiration.

Brand Identity Style Guide Lookbook Visual Direction

The lookbook and style guide are the deliverables. It's a complete brand world built from nothing, which is a useful thing to show because it means none of the decisions were inherited.
