The Cake Co. order in, automation on.

A custom cake studio was losing customers in the silence between form submission and follow-up. We built a four-email automated sequence that handles the whole order lifecycle, coded in HTML and loaded directly into MailerLite.

Concept project Order automation Email sequence MailerLite Square

The problem

the gap nobody talks about.

Custom order businesses have a very specific problem. Someone finds them, gets excited, fills out the form — and then hears nothing. Not because the baker isn't interested. Because she's busy baking. By the time she circles back, the customer has either moved on or lost confidence.

The fix isn't working faster. It's making sure the right message goes out automatically, at the right moment, every time. No chasing, no dropped balls, no awkward "sorry for the delay" emails.

The Cake Co. is a fictional custom cake studio in the Annapolis Valley. We built their full order automation as a working example of what this looks like when it's done properly — from the first confirmation email all the way through to delivery day.

The stack

four tools, one clean flow.

Every tool here is something a small business owner can set up, afford, and actually use. Nothing enterprise, nothing overkill.

Order intake

Jotform

Custom order form captures occasion, servings, flavour, delivery details, and design notes. All of it feeds directly into the automation.

Connection layer

Zapier

When a form is submitted, Zapier adds the contact to MailerLite and triggers the sequence. One connection, set up once.

Email automation

MailerLite

Coded HTML templates loaded directly into MailerLite. The automation runs the sequence, times the sends, and handles the logic.

Payment

Square

The quote email includes a Square invoice link. Customer pays the deposit, Square sends their receipt, date is locked.

The flow

what happens and when.

Four emails. One manual step. Everything else runs on its own.

The one decision

how the deposit confirmation triggers.

Everything in this sequence is automated except one step: the quote. Pricing a custom cake requires a human eye, so that email gets sent manually after the baker reviews the order. Everything else runs on its own.

The deposit confirmation (email 2b) depends on Square knowing the payment cleared. There are two ways to handle that, and the right answer depends on volume.

Option A

Fully connected

Zapier watches Square for a completed payment. When the deposit clears, Zapier triggers the confirmation email in MailerLite automatically. The customer gets it within minutes of paying. Slightly more setup, and Zapier at this task volume runs around $20 per month.

Best for: higher volume, or when speed of confirmation matters.

Option B

One manual step

When the Square payment notification comes through on her phone, she goes into MailerLite and manually triggers the confirmation for that contact. Takes about 30 seconds. Saves the Zapier cost entirely. For a business doing 10 to 20 custom orders a month, this is the honest recommendation.

Best for: lower volume, or keeping overhead lean.

Good automation design knows which steps need a human and which don't. The goal isn't full automation for its own sake. It's removing the friction that actually costs you customers.

The sequence

four emails. coded and ready to load.

Every email below is a working HTML template built for MailerLite. The order details populate automatically from the Jotform submission via merge tags. The customer always sees their own information reflected back to them, not a generic placeholder.
