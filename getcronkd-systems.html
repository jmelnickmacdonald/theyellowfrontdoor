Get Cronk’d is a boutique fitness studio in Kemptville, Ontario. Nine years in business. Owner Rebecca Cronk is a UFE Pro Wellness World Champion with a full roster of group classes, personal training, and specialty programs.

We built a registration and operations system from scratch inside Airtable, confirmation emails, class rosters, client tracking, renewal flags, and a marketing layer, at a fraction of the cost of a dedicated platform subscription. The demo data below is fictional. The system is not.

The stack

one tool, the whole operation.

The entire system lives inside Airtable. The registration form, the client base, and the dashboard all read from the same data. Nothing needs a second tool to talk to a first one.

Registration

Airtable form

Clients register directly through an Airtable-embedded form on the website. Class selection, contact details, package type. On submit, the record lands in the base and the dashboard updates immediately.

Confirmation

Airtable automation

A native Airtable automation sends a confirmation email the moment a new record is created. No Zapier, no third-party email tool. The form submits, the record is created, and the email goes out. All inside one workspace.

Operations and marketing

Dashboard

Rebecca opens one view and sees the full week’s schedule with expandable rosters, session counts per client, payment status, renewal flags for lapsing members, and pre-built marketing segments ready to connect to an email tool when she wants to activate them.

A note on payments: Payments run through the studio’s existing system, and that part wasn’t broken. If bringing everything into one place becomes the goal, Stripe connects to Airtable and the path is short.

Airtable’s free tier handles this comfortably for a studio of this size. The paid plan, if needed, runs around $20 a month. The gap between that and a dedicated platform subscription is not the tools. It is knowing what to build and how to connect it.

Step 0 of 4

A client registers. The whole system starts here.

The registration form on Get Cronk’d’s website is an Airtable form embedded directly on the page. No separate form tool. No integration to set up and maintain. The client fills it in, submits, and a record is created in the Airtable base immediately.

What you see on the right is the actual registration page layout — the same form that is live on the site, embedded the same way, with the same sidebar cards.

Why this matters

Most studio registration systems live in a separate platform from the management system. A staff member manually copies registrations across. Here the form and the database are the same thing. There is no transfer step and no room for anything to fall through.

Step 1 of 4

Linda gets a confirmation before she closes the tab.

The moment the form submits, an Airtable automation fires a confirmation email. Class details, what to bring, what to expect as a first-timer. It reads like Rebecca wrote it personally.

Rebecca did not write it for Linda specifically. She wrote it once. Airtable sends it every time a new registration comes in, personalised with the client’s name and class details pulled directly from the record.

Why this matters

A new client who just signed up for a 6am class has a small window of doubt between registering and showing up. The confirmation closes it. It tells them their spot is held, what to expect, and that someone is expecting them. That email is the difference between a trial client who comes in and one who quietly does not.

Trigger Airtable detects a new record in the registrations base and fires the confirmation automation. No Zapier, no third-party tool. All inside one workspace.

Step 3 of 4

The client list tells Rebecca who needs attention before they disappear.

The Clients tab shows every member with their session count, payment status, how long they have been coming, and a flag when they are at risk. At risk means they have been coming less frequently or their package is nearly out and they have not renewed.

Rebecca does not have to notice these things herself. The dashboard surfaces them. Use the filter buttons to sort by status.

Why this matters

The clients most likely to quietly stop coming are the ones nobody notices drifting. A member who used to come three times a week and is now coming once is at risk. By the time they cancel, the relationship is already over. Catching it two weeks earlier means there is still time to reach out.

How it works Airtable calculates each client’s session count and attendance from their registration records. The at-risk flag is a formula field. No manual review required.

Step 4 of 4

The marketing segments are built. The emails are one connection away.

The Marketing tab has five pre-built segments: renewal nudges, lapsed member re-engagement, at-risk outreach, birthday notes, and free trial follow-ups. Each segment pulls automatically from the client base.

Right now Rebecca reviews these and reaches out when she has time. Connecting them to MailerLite or Brevo makes them fire automatically. The segments are already built. That step is one Zapier connection.

Why this matters

The value of a marketing automation layer is not the technology. It is knowing which clients to talk to, when, and what to say. That thinking is already done here. Turning it on is not a project — it is an afternoon.
