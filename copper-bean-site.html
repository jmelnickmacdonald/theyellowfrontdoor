Our Story

The house got quiet.
So we did something
about it.

We are Steve and Alexa. Between us we've raised four kids, held two corporate jobs with matching pension plans, and spent more years than we'd like to count commuting to offices with our names on the parking spots. When the youngest kiddo left for university, the house became eerily quiet, and we looked at each other across a kitchen that suddenly felt too big and said, "What if we just didn't do this anymore?" So we didn't.

We'd trade the pension
for this again
any day.

We cashed in the stability, found a space just down the road from home, spent a chunk of change on paint and lumber, and a short six weeks later we opened a coffee bar. The coffee is significantly better than what we were drinking at our desks; the hours are significantly worse. But we get to build something with our hands, serve people food we're proud of, meet the best people and be home by four — which is more than either of us could say before.

We're particular about what we serve, but not in a super uptight, pretentious way (promise). We buy great coffee beans from people we actually know, we make everything in the kitchen that morning, and the menu is short on purpose — we'd rather do a few things properly than fill a board with fluff.

Come in. Grab the seat by the window.
We'll take it from here.

What We're Making

The menu.

The menu is purposely limited. Picking from a big menu is stressful, okay? Especially before coffee. Everything on it is made here that morning, the coffee is always fresh, and we change things up with the seasons. If it's on the board, it's good. If it's gone, someone else thought it was good earlier in the day.

The Squash Bowl

Roasted squash, pickled onion, toasted seeds, and tahini. It's one of those meals that just makes you feel like everything's going to be fine. People order it once and then it becomes their usual.

The Egg Sandwich

Fried egg, melty cheddar, a little chili oil, greens, and a toasted bun that holds the whole thing together. It's the kind of sandwich you'd make at home if you had the energy at 7 a.m., which you don't, so we make it for you.

The Daily Soup

Whatever sounded good this morning, made from scratch with whatever looked best at the market. We write it on the board when it's ready. Comes with bread that's still warm, because cold bread with soup is a crime.

The Pastry Case

Croissants, scones, muffins, sometimes something we haven't made before just to see what happens. There's always butter involved. By noon it's usually picked clean, so if you see something you like, grab it.

Coffee

We care about it a lot, but we're not going to quiz you. Espresso, drip, pour-over — tell us how you like it and we'll make it that way. Oat milk, regular milk, whatever you want. No judgement, no surcharge.

Tea & Other Things

Loose-leaf tea, hot chocolate that's actually made with real chocolate, a couple of fresh juices, and sparkling water for the people who need something cold. We keep it simple because a menu shouldn't require a decision tree.

The menu changes with the seasons and sometimes just with the morning. Whatever's on the board is what we've got.
