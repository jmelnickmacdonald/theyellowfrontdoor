Our Story

The house got quiet.
So we did something
about it.

Between us we've raised four kids, held two corporate jobs with matching pension plans, and spent more years than we'd like to count commuting to offices with our names on the parking spots. Then the last one left for university, the house went quiet, and we looked at each other across a kitchen that suddenly felt too big and said, "What if we just didn't do this anymore?" So we didn't.

We'd trade the pension
for this again
tomorrow.

We cashed in the stability, found a space on Gottingen Street, and opened a coffee bar. The coffee is significantly better than what we were drinking at our desks; the hours are significantly worse. But we get to build something with our hands, serve people food we're proud of, and be home by five — which is more than either of us could say before.

We're particular about what we serve, but not in a super uptight, pretentious way (promise). We buy great coffee beans from people we actually know, we make everything in the kitchen that morning, and the menu stays short on purpose — we'd rather do a few things properly than fill a board with things we're not excited about.

Sit anywhere.
Order at the counter.
Stay as long as you like.

What We're Making

The menu.

The menu is intentionally limited. Picking from a big menu is stressful, okay? Especially before coffee. Everything on it is made here that morning, the coffee is fresh, and we change things up with the seasons. If it's on the board, it's good. If it's gone, it was good earlier.

The Squash Bowl

Roasted squash, pickled onion, seeds, and tahini. It's warm, it's filling, and people keep ordering it, so we keep making it.

The Egg Sandwich

Fried egg, cheddar, a little chili oil, greens, toasted bun. Nothing fancy going on here; it just works.

The Pastry Case

Changes every morning depending on what we felt like baking. Usually involves butter. Usually gone by noon. If you see something you want, don't overthink it.

Coffee

We take it seriously but we're not weird about it. Espresso, drip, or pour-over; we've got oat milk and regular milk and nobody's going to judge your order.

The menu changes with the seasons and sometimes just with the morning. Whatever's on the board is what we've got.
