Concept · Brand Identity

Lydia Nichols

Lydia doesn't exist, but the problem she represents does. We've talked to enough real estate agents in this province to know that most of them are great at their jobs and invisible in their branding. Same navy. Same headshot. Same "call me for all your real estate needs." So we built the agent we kept wishing existed and gave her a brand to match.

Logo

We wanted something that felt personal without being cutesy. The arch monogram is a nod to doorways and thresholds — which felt right for a real estate brand — without being on the nose about it. The botanical detail softens it just enough. It scales well, which matters when the same mark has to live on a business card and a newsletter header.

Colour

The palette came from the landscape more than anything else. Off-white and black as the base, clean and uncluttered, letting the photography breathe. The dusty blue and lupin purple are pulled straight from a Nova Scotia June: that particular shade of sky and the purple that shows up along every back road for about three weeks a year. The gold only appears on the business card as a foil treatment on cream stock, which is exactly where it should be. Something you notice when you're holding it, not something plastered across every touchpoint.

Tagline

"Elevated service. Homegrown in the Valley." We liked that the two halves push against each other slightly. Elevated implies polish. Homegrown implies the opposite. But that's actually Lydia — someone who knows every back road and still shows up prepared.

What we built

Logo suite, colour palette, business card designed for gold foil on cream stock, a monthly newsletter template, social templates for listings, and a door knocker in two versions.
