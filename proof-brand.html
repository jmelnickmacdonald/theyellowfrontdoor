Fin & Oak Brand Guide

Brand Book · Voice & Tone · Identity System

A complete brand system for a mom-and-daughter pet bakery in the Annapolis Valley. The guide covers the logo, the colours, the typography, the voice rules, and the things the brand would never say. All written so that anyone picking it up three years from now can keep it consistent without asking.

The test for every rule in here was the same: does this sound like the people who actually run this bakery?

Voice Guide · Messaging · Word List · Sample Copy

Voice came first. Who are Steve and Alexa, how do they talk, and what would they never say? The guide defines the brand in four words, shows what it sounds like versus what it doesn't, and includes a word list that acts as a fence around the voice. The most useful guardrail in here isn't a rule. It's a single example of what Copper + Bean would never sound like.

Concept · Brand Identity

Raven House Atelier

Brand Identity · Style Guide · Lookbook

A personal project built to explore what a quiet, craft-focused leather goods brand could look and feel like. The imagery is AI-generated and the whole identity was designed from nothing as an exercise in restraint. The voice is quiet on purpose. It talks about materials and craft, not lifestyle or aspiration.

The lookbook and style guide are the deliverables. It's a complete brand world built from scratch, which means none of the decisions were inherited.

Concept · Email Design

Lydia Nichols

Email Newsletter · Brand Voice · Visual Design

A referral email newsletter for a fictional Annapolis Valley real estate agent, designed to feel like something worth opening. The brief was to make it look and sound like a person: warm, grounded in place, and completely unlike the average real estate email blast.

The lupin field header isn't decoration. It's Lydia's brand in a single image. Rural Nova Scotia, quietly beautiful, unmistakably local.
