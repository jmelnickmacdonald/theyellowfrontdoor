Section 03

Pin Down What Matters

A practical intake page to help me understand what you are looking for, what matters most, and what home needs to do for your life.

In this section

  • Preferred areas, property type, budget comfort, and timing.
  • Must-haves, nice-to-haves, and dealbreakers.
  • Lifestyle details that can shape the search.
  • A note area for questions or anything that needs more context.

The main idea

The better I understand the life around the home, the better I can help you search.

Bedrooms and budget matter, but so do routines, commute, privacy, storage, pets, family, work, and the way you want home to feel.

Start the Form

Before the search narrows

Let’s separate what matters from what would simply be nice.

Most buyers begin with a mix of needs, hopes, and things they are still figuring out. That is normal. This section helps us sort those ideas before the search gets too narrow.

You do not need perfect answers. A rough answer is still helpful. The goal is to give us a clearer starting point so the homes we view are more aligned with your life, not just your search filters.

Helpful reminder: a must-have should be something that truly affects whether the home works for you. A nice-to-have is something you would love, but could compromise on if the rest of the home made sense.

Buyer intake

What are you looking for?

Use this as a starting point. Your answers are saved on this device as you go.

Think layout, bedrooms, accessibility, outdoor space, parking, school area, commute, privacy, pets, or anything that would be hard to compromise on.

After you fill this out

We can use this to make the search more useful.

Once we know what matters most, we can make better decisions about which listings are worth viewing, which compromises are reasonable, and which homes look good online but may not fit your life in practice.

This also gives us a clearer way to talk after showings. Instead of asking whether a home was simply “nice,” we can ask whether it actually matched what you said mattered.

Your note

Have a question, concern, or detail you want me to know? Add it here. You can keep it on this device, or choose to share this specific note with me before we talk.
