Buyer Guide

The Nuts and Bolts of Buying a Home

A practical guide to help you understand the process, get organized, and feel more prepared as we start looking together.

Start here

A clear place to begin.

Buying a home comes with a lot of moving parts, but you do not need to figure them all out at once. This guide is here to help you understand the process, sort your thoughts, and know what to expect as we move from early questions to actual showings.

Use the sections in whatever order makes sense. Some are for early planning, some are for viewing homes, and some are for when we are getting closer to an offer.

How to use this guide

  • Start with the section that matches where you are right now.
  • You can come back to any part when it becomes relevant.
  • Use the notes to keep track of questions or details you want to talk through.
  • You can keep notes for yourself or choose to share a specific note with me.

Your notes help make our conversations better.

As you move through the guide, you may have questions, concerns, or details you want me to know. You can keep those notes for yourself, or choose to share a specific note with me before we talk.

The goal is not to make this feel formal. It is simply a way to make sure the important things do not get lost between texts, listings, showings, and decisions.

Example note: “I’m not sure how firm I am on location yet. I like the idea of being closer to town, but I also want more privacy. Can we talk through that before we narrow the search too much?”

You do not need to have it all figured out before we talk.

That is what the first conversation is for. Bring your questions, your rough ideas, your wish list, your concerns, or even just the feeling that you may be ready to start. We can sort through it together.
